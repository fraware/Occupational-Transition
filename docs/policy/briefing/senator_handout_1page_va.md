# Virginia AI and Labor: one-page handout

## What public federal data says now (Virginia)

- Virginia's retained 2025 Q3 benchmark profile is concentrated in:
  - Health care and social assistance: **37.62%**
  - Manufacturing: **28.69%**
- Peer-relative context in retained state set:
  - Retail share rank: **1 of 31**
  - Manufacturing share rank: **4 of 31**
- This evidence supports immediate oversight monitoring, not causal claims.

## Why this matters for Senate action

- Virginia's sector mix suggests national averages alone can miss state-specific
  adjustment pressure.
- Existing public instruments already provide a defensible monitoring stack:
  structural benchmark + peer comparison + rank profile + KPI dashboard.
- Federal policy can move now by upgrading existing surveys rather than waiting
  for a full greenfield redesign.

## Three federal asks (phased)

- **0-3 months:** finalize CPS AI worker module language, stabilize BTOS core
  AI items, and lock publication metadata standards.
- **3-9 months:** field and publish recurring CPS/BTOS outputs with transparent
  methods notes.
- **9-18 months:** launch scoped JOLTS supplement pilot for occupation-aware
  demand context.
