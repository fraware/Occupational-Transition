# Figure 2 — Worker hours and mobility (CPS)

**What is plotted.** **Panel A** reports weighted mean usual weekly hours by month and AI-relevance tercile for employed CPS respondents with valid hours and occupation. **Panel B** combines a heatmap of weighted transition counts for the latest matched month with time-series lines for summary transition probabilities: retention, occupation switching, unemployment entry, and NILF entry.

**How to read it.** In Panel A, compare vertical gaps between tercile lines within each month and compare slopes over time. In Panel B, darker heatmap cells indicate larger latest-month flows. Each line chart tracks one row-normalized transition metric across months.

**Interpretation.** The figure provides a descriptive worker-side view of hours levels and broad labor-force and occupation mobility patterns by AI-relevance tercile in recent CPS data.

**Boundary.** These are descriptive matched-month survey constructs and do not identify employer AI adoption, worker-firm linkage, or causal AI effects on hours or transitions.

Alt-text: Two-panel figure. Panel A is a multi-line chart of weighted mean weekly hours over time by AI-relevance tercile. Panel B combines a latest-month transition-count heatmap with small time-series charts for retention, occupation switching, unemployment entry, and NILF entry.
